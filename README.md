# AI for Real Life

Simple systems for the things that actually matter, with AI as a quiet helper where it earns its place (and left out where it doesn't). No app to live inside, no productivity religion, no shame. Build one small system at a time until your life mostly runs itself — starting with the one almost everyone neglects: **keeping up with the people you love.**

I'm Mick. This is the calm, do-less version of getting organized. Start with one system this week. That's the whole method. [Who's Mick? →](#whos-mick)

## The idea
Most "get organized" advice hands you a fifty-step system and a guilt complex. This is the opposite. A *system* is just a small decision you make once so you stop re-deciding it forever. Autopay is a system. A weekly meal plan is a system. "Text one old friend every Sunday" is a system. You do not need all of them. You need a few good ones, added slowly.

The framework here is organized around a handful of life areas (health, mind, money, environment, and — pulled to the front — **relationships**). Browse them in **[The Systems](systems/the-systems.md)**, steal what fits, ignore the rest.

> The one conviction underneath all of it: relationships come first. Staying in touch is the system almost everyone neglects, and it is the one that quietly makes or breaks a life.

## What this is (and isn't)
- **Is:** free, calm, one-small-thing-at-a-time. For real people with full lives.
- **Isn't:** a second-brain to maintain, a subscription, or a promise that a system will fix you. Add one. See if life gets a little easier.
