# The Weekly Meal Plan

> Decide the week's dinners once, so the daily 5pm "what's for dinner" question stops costing you. · **Payoff:** Time back · **Setup:** about 15 minutes a week

**Why it's worth it.** The exhausting part of dinner is not the cooking, it is the *deciding*, every single evening, while tired and hungry. Decide once for the week and you pay that tax a single time instead of seven. You eat better, spend less, and stop the 6pm takeout default.

**The setup (once, then a weekly beat).**
1. Pick a repeatable slot to plan: same fifteen minutes each week (Sunday morning works).
2. Choose dinners for the nights you will actually cook. Not seven. Aim for four or five and leave room for leftovers and a night off.
3. Lean on a short rotation of meals you already know. Novelty is where meal plans go to die.
4. Turn the plan into one grocery list and shop once.

**Where AI earns its place (and where it doesn't).** This is a spot where AI genuinely helps. Give it your constraints ("five weeknight dinners, no pork, nothing over 30 minutes, using what's already in my fridge: ...") and it will draft a week and a grouped shopping list in seconds. Let it handle the blank-page generation. Keep for yourself the part that matters: whether your household will actually eat it. Taste is not a prompt.

**Keep it small.** On a full week, shrink to deciding *just tomorrow's* dinner tonight. Even one decision made in advance beats standing in front of the fridge at 6pm.
