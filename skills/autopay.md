# Autopay the Safe Stuff

> Put the bills you can safely automate on autopay, once, and stop the monthly scramble. · **Payoff:** Peace of mind · **Setup:** about 20 minutes, once

**Why it's worth it.** Late fees and due-date anxiety are almost never a money problem. They are a *remembering* problem, and remembering is exactly what a system replaces. Automate the safe, predictable bills once and you delete a recurring low-grade worry for good.

**The setup (once).**
1. List your recurring bills. Split them: fixed and predictable (rent/mortgage, insurance, subscriptions, phone) versus variable or worth eyeballing (credit card, utilities that spike).
2. Put the fixed, predictable ones on autopay from the biller's site or your bank.
3. For the variable ones, autopay the *minimum* (so you are never late) and keep paying the rest by hand after a glance.
4. Set one calendar note a couple of days after payday to confirm the balance can cover it. That is the whole ongoing job.

**Where AI earns its place (and where it doesn't).** Be honest: AI mostly sits this one out. Autopay is a setting on a website, not something a chatbot does for you, and you should never hand an assistant your banking logins. The one fair use is upstream: paste an anonymized list of your subscriptions and ask which look redundant or overpriced before you automate them. Automating a bill you should have cancelled just makes the leak quieter.

**Keep it small.** If twenty minutes feels like too much, do the single biggest or most-often-late bill today and stop. One automated bill is one less thing to carry.
