# The Keep-in-Touch System

The relationships in your life do not fade because you stopped caring. They fade because nobody was in charge of the *rhythm*, and weeks quietly become years. This system puts something small in charge of the rhythm, so caring is enough.

## The core idea: people have a cadence
You already sense it. Some people you want in your life weekly. Some monthly. Some you would be glad to catch once a year, and that is right for them. The problem is never the feeling — it is that "I should reach out" has no trigger, so it never fires.

Give each person a cadence and let a simple tool watch the clock. Then you are never scanning your whole life wondering who you have neglected. You open the [Keep in Touch tool](../tool/index.html), see who is overdue, and reach out. That is it. Everything stays on your device; this is a private rhythm, not a social network.

## Setting it up (ten minutes, once)
1. List the people you keep meaning to reach and losing track of. Start with ten, not a hundred.
2. Give each a cadence that feels *generous, not guilt-inducing* — the rhythm you would be happy to keep, not the one you think you "should."
3. If you remember roughly when you last talked, add it. If not, leave it blank; they will show as "new" and you can start fresh.
4. Once a week, open the tool, see who is overdue, and send one message. Mark them done. That is the whole maintenance.

## The part most people miss: reach out *about something*
"Hey, long time, how are you" is the reason keeping in touch feels like a chore — it is a blank page every time. Systematize the *reason*, not just the reminder:
- **Do a thing together, apart.** Send a puzzle, a crossword, a daily word game, and compare times. A standing "who got today's puzzle faster" thread keeps a friendship warm with zero small talk.
- **The forward.** Keep a mental note of who loves what. See a thing they would love — an article, a recipe, a stupid video — and it becomes the message. No "how are you" required.
- **The specific callback.** "You mentioned your mom's surgery — how did it go?" beats "how's life" every time. Jot the specific thing when you hear it.
- **The standing date.** The strongest system of all: a recurring thing on the calendar (monthly call, a walk, game night) so there is nothing to initiate at all.

## Why cadence beats intensity
You do not need grand gestures. A four-line message on a reliable rhythm does more for a friendship than a heartfelt reconnection every three years. Showing up small and often is the whole secret, and it is a system, not a personality trait. Anyone can run it.
